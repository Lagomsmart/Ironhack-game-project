      astringency
      /strinj()ns/
      noun
      noun: astringency; plural noun: astringencies
      1. 1.
        the property of causing skin cells or other body tissues to contract.
        "witch hazel is valued for its astringency and its toning properties"
      2. 2.
        slight acidity or bitterness of taste or smell.
        "you can add a bag of fruit-flavored tea to the cup to minimize the astringency of green tea"
      3. 3.
        sharpness or severity in manner or style.
        "the astringency of his personality"
      Origin
      early 17th century: from astringent + -ency.
